## CONSOLIDATED FINDINGS

### Full partner journey (sign-up → first $)
1. **Land on ulio.ai** from creator content (TikTok/IG reels from taped-mouth guy @wifi.racks and his affiliate network).
2. **Click "Start Earning"** → `/signup` → enter Business Name + Email + Password → **"Create account and subscribe"** (paid — early access, price undisclosed pre-auth).
3. Land in **Partner Dashboard** with sidebar: Dashboard / Businesses / Find Leads / Analytics / Branding / Resources / Support / Settings.
4. Click **Find Leads** → pick industry + location → get scored lead grid (refreshed daily). Each lead comes with score 0-100, reviews, business size, opportunity %.
5. Click a lead → **Business Deep Dive** panel enriched with Owner name, Email, Direct Line, Website, Hours, Est. Revenue + 4-6 more data points + PHONE ISSUES MENTIONED IN REVIEWS analysis.
6. Click **+ Add Business** → fill Business Name, Business Phone, Time Zone, Owner (optional), Owner Email → set Pricing & Billing (Platform Billing toggle ON = Ulio handles payments) → drag Monthly Price slider + Estimated Call Minutes slider → Ulio shows live Est. Cost / Est. Profit / "Profitable" badge.
7. **Instant Builder — One-Click Deploy** — pick from AI Products to Deploy: AI Receptionist, Chatbot, Website. AI trains on business data automatically.
8. **Agent config — Brain tab:** AI Greeting, Behavior, Knowledge Base (import from website or upload files). Tabs: Brain / Tests / Go Live / Business.
9. **Tests tab:** test via chat + voice (purple "Tap to Start" mic).
10. **Proposal Builder (AI-Generated):** auto-creates a Full AI Business Suite proposal at $497/mo (configurable), bundles AI Receptionist + Website + Chatbot + 24/7 Coverage, shows annual revenue. Copy example: *"While you're focused on making a neighbor's home shine, your phone is leaking $100,000 a year in untouched revenue."*
11. Click **"Send Proposal"** → email/link goes to business owner.
12. Business pays via Ulio's Platform Billing → Ulio collects, keeps the COGS ($0.03/min-ish on minutes), pays partner the margin ($300-$500/mo typical).
13. Partner watches Revenue Overview on dashboard — example stats: $138k/mo, 319 businesses, $432.90 avg/business.

### Lead Finder — how it works
- Input: industry + city + (optional niche).
- Source: Google Maps / scraped listings (not confirmed, but data points shown — ratings, reviews, hours, website — are classic Google Business Profile fields).
- Enrichment: per-lead Business Deep Dive surfaces ~6 data points (Owner, Email, Direct Line, Website, Hours, Est. Revenue).
- Scoring: 0-100 composite using industry, business size, review health, opportunity score, and mined phone-issue mentions in reviews.
- Tags: High Volume, Private Practice, Phone Issues in Reviews, Service Issues Mentioned, Below 4.0 Rating.
- "Sorted by opportunity" default. Refreshed daily ("Hundreds of leads refreshed daily").
- Claim: surfaces "businesses searching for AI phone solutions" (marketing), but in practice it seems to score based on review signals that imply phone issues (e.g., complaints about not being able to reach the business).

### Deploy engine — how it works
- "One-click deploy" builds 3 products automatically:
  - **AI Receptionist** (voice agent — answers calls 24/7, uses pre-installed voice + behavior, KB auto-imported from business website).
  - **Chatbot** (website chat widget, trained same way).
  - **Website** (AI-generated landing page — template-based with business branding).
- AI trains itself on business data (scraped website + uploaded files).
- Agent config has pre-set Behavior + AI Greeting. Partner mostly doesn't configure from zero.

### Proposal engine — how it works
- Auto-generated the moment Instant Builder finishes.
- Uses business name, industry, and "pain point" from review mining in copy (e.g., "$100k a year in untouched revenue" framing).
- Bundle: Full AI Business Suite = AI Receptionist + Website + Chatbot + 24/7 Coverage tag.
- Fields: Package, Monthly Price (default $497 in landing demo), Proposal Ready status.
- "Sending..." state then presumably emails/texts business owner a branded proposal with checkout link.
- Partner's branding applied (see white-label).

### UX patterns worth copying
1. **Live profitability math** in the pricing form. Slider for price + slider for minutes → Est. Cost + Est. Profit + "Profitable" badge recompute in real time. Partners never have to guess margin.
2. **Lead scoring with review-signal tags** (Phone Issues in Reviews, Below 4.0 Rating, High Volume). Makes picking leads trivial.
3. **"Business Deep Dive" enrichment panel** — Owner, Email, Direct Line, Website, Hours, Est. Revenue surfaced *before* you deploy = reduces friction.
4. **Three-step hero ("Find. Build. Bill. Done.")** — marketing copy is tight, benefit-forward, no jargon.
5. **Content Library for affiliates** — Ulio hands creators 18+ ready-made assets (demo videos, swipe files, ROI calculators, carousel PSDs). That's how they go viral. This is a growth machine Renée should clone.
6. **White-label from day 1** — "Your Brand" card on landing page + custom subdomain. Removes the "but this isn't my company" objection.
7. **Platform Billing toggle** ("we handle payments from this business") — removes partner's biggest operational headache: collecting money.
8. **Instant Builder status states** — "Building...", "Ready to build", "Active Lead", "Sending..." micro-state animations reinforce the "AI is working for you" vibe.
9. **Dashboard nostalgia** — 14-Month Revenue Trend chart + Total Earnings (All Time) with % growth pill is pure gamification.
10. **"Send Proposal" as the atomic unit of work** — every action chains to a proposal. Partner's job literally becomes hitting one button.

### UX patterns to IMPROVE on for Easy Deploy
1. **"Partners don't own their accounts" (red flag from Instagram comment).** Build Renée's Easy Deploy so partners own the GHL sub-account, own the Twilio number, own the data — give them export/takeover rights. Beat Ulio on ownership.
2. **Lead-finder relies on review-scraping.** That's scrape-ToS risky (Google) and fragile (reviews change). Renée should also pull from **LinkedIn Sales Nav + public filings + permit data** (for trades) for more durable signal.
3. **Proposal copy is formulaic** ("$100k leaking revenue"). Renée's Lex (Copy) can generate industry-specific hooks with actual local data instead of templates.
4. **Zero mention of compliance** on Ulio's site. No CASL/TCPA/DNC language, no A2P 10DLC reference for SMS, no PIPEDA for Canada. Renée can position Canadian-grade compliance as a feature, especially for Connor (Compliance) checks.
5. **No voice customization shown** — "AI voice + behavior comes pre-installed" is sold as a feature but it's actually a limitation. Let Easy Deploy partners clone the business owner's own voice with ElevenLabs Voice Clone as an upsell — huge differentiator.
6. **No CRM write-back.** Ulio AI answers calls but the lead data seemingly stays inside Ulio. Renée's GHL-native build means every call → lead → opportunity is in the partner's CRM from second one. Massive.
7. **No live-transfer / rescue call.** Ulio's agent is fully autonomous. Add "AI answers, but escalates to a human when intent score > X" as a default — service businesses trust this more.
8. **No industry templates.** Ulio has one generic receptionist template. Renée should ship with 10+ industry blueprints (mortgage, dental, HVAC, roofing, realtor) preloaded with intents + FAQs + booking flows.
9. **Slow voice test loop.** Partner has to click, speak, wait, read. Renée could add "simulated call" — AI plays the caller and stress-tests the agent against 20 scripted scenarios automatically.
10. **Signup is paywalled with no free trial shown.** Bad for conversion. Easy Deploy should offer "deploy first client free, pay only when business says yes."
11. **Affiliate program is US-centric** (Stripe + PayPal). Canadian partners need Wise / Canadian-bank payouts. Small thing, but Renée's market.
12. **Dashboard looks great but is single-user.** No team seats, no role-based access. An agency with 2-5 operators would feel constrained. Add roles/seats from day 1.
