## 1. WHY THIS SKILL EXISTS

Easy Deploy is a SaaS platform that lets partner-agencies deploy AI automation (voice AI, SMS, email) for end-businesses (dentist, plumber, realtor, mortgage broker, etc.). That means risk flows three ways:

1. **End-business → their customers** — if the end-business uses the system to contact their own customers without consent, THE END-BUSINESS is liable under CASL/TCPA/GDPR. But Xpert needs contractual clarity that this is the end-business's responsibility, not Xpert's.
2. **Partner → end-business** — if the partner-agency promises things Xpert can't deliver, Xpert needs to be insulated.
3. **Xpert platform operational risks** — downtime, data breach, AI error, regulatory change.

The disclaimer language below creates the contractual guardrails. Again — these are **drafts for a lawyer to finalize**.

## 2. CORE DISCLAIMER BLOCK — THE SHORT VERSION

This is the block that goes in the footer of every proposal, onboarding email, and receipt. Under 200 words. Plain English.

```
IMPORTANT DISCLOSURES — Easy Deploy / Xpert Web Solutions Inc.

1. YOUR COMPLIANCE IS YOURS. You are solely responsible for ensuring your use of
   this system complies with all laws in your country, state, province, and industry —
   including but not limited to CASL (Canada), TCPA (US), GDPR (EU), and any state,
   provincial, or industry-specific privacy, advertising, or telecommunications rules.
   We provide the tool; you operate it.

2. AI DISCLOSURE. Communications generated by this system use AI. Some jurisdictions
   require you to disclose this to your end-users. Knowing where that applies is
   your responsibility.

3. NO OUTCOME GUARANTEE. We do not guarantee specific revenue, leads, appointments,
   conversion rates, or business outcomes. Examples we share reflect past results and
   are not promises.

4. YOUR DATA, YOUR CONSENT. You are responsible for the accuracy of all data you load
   into the system (phone numbers, contact lists, lead sources) and for obtaining
   proper consent from your contacts before any outreach.

5. TERMINATION FOR MISUSE. We may suspend or terminate service if the system is used
   for spam, fraud, harassment, or illegal activity.

6. JURISDICTION. This agreement is governed by the laws of Ontario, Canada unless
   your signed agreement specifies otherwise.

Xpert Web Solutions Inc. — Toronto, Ontario, Canada.
Full terms: {{custom_values.terms_url}}
```

Tokens: `{{custom_values.terms_url}}` — link to the full Terms of Service (see §7).

## 3. THE LONG VERSION — FOR THE SIGNED CONTRACT

This block appears in the body of the master service agreement and the e-sign flow at checkout. 10 clauses, numbered for easy reference during contract negotiation.

### 3.1 Compliance — client owns it

```
3.1 COMPLIANCE RESPONSIBILITY

You ("Client") acknowledge and agree that you are solely responsible for ensuring
your use of the Easy Deploy system (the "Platform") complies with all applicable laws
in every country, state, province, and industry in which you operate. This includes
but is not limited to:

(a) Canada: Canada's Anti-Spam Legislation (CASL); Personal Information Protection and
    Electronic Documents Act (PIPEDA); Canadian Radio-television and Telecommunications
    Commission (CRTC) Unsolicited Telecommunications Rules; provincial privacy
    statutes (e.g., Alberta PIPA, BC PIPA, Quebec Law 25); industry-specific statutes
    including — where applicable — Financial Services Regulatory Authority of Ontario
    (FSRA) advertising rules for mortgage professionals;

(b) United States: Telephone Consumer Protection Act (TCPA); CAN-SPAM Act; state-level
    privacy laws including but not limited to CCPA/CPRA (California), CPA (Colorado),
    CTDPA (Connecticut), UCPA (Utah), VCDPA (Virginia); and any state-level
    auto-dialer, AI-voice, or Do-Not-Call regulation;

(c) European Union & UK: General Data Protection Regulation (GDPR); UK GDPR; ePrivacy
    Directive; any national implementation thereof;

(d) Any industry-specific regulatory framework applicable to your business, including
    but not limited to: securities and insurance regulation; medical, dental, or
    health-information privacy rules (HIPAA in the US, PHIPA in Ontario, equivalents
    elsewhere); real estate regulatory boards; mortgage brokerage regulators.

Xpert Web Solutions Inc. ("Xpert", "we") provides the tool. You operate it. You are
solely responsible for determining which laws apply to your business, and for
complying with them.
```

### 3.2 Consent for end-user outreach

```
3.2 CONSENT FROM YOUR CONTACTS

You represent and warrant that every phone number, email address, and contact record
loaded into or used through the Platform has a documented, lawful basis for contact
under the applicable law of the contact's jurisdiction. This includes obtaining
express or implied consent as required by CASL, TCPA, GDPR, or equivalent framework.

You are solely responsible for:
(a) maintaining records of consent;
(b) honoring unsubscribe and Do-Not-Call requests within the legally required window;
(c) scrubbing lists against applicable Do-Not-Call registries;
(d) maintaining an internal suppression list for opt-outs;
(e) refreshing consent documentation where required.

If you do not have a lawful basis to contact someone, you must not contact them
through the Platform. Xpert is not your consent-management service.
```

### 3.3 AI disclosure

```
3.3 AI-GENERATED COMMUNICATIONS

The Platform uses artificial intelligence to generate voice, text, and email
communications. Various jurisdictions require or recommend disclosure that a
communication is AI-generated — for example, CRTC guidance (Telecom Regulatory
Policy CRTC 2023-150 and related), US Federal Trade Commission guidance on AI voice,
California's AB 3211 and related, and emerging EU AI Act obligations.

You are solely responsible for:
(a) knowing which AI-disclosure obligations apply to your use of the Platform;
(b) configuring your Platform agents to make legally required disclosures;
(c) keeping current as this area of law evolves.

Xpert provides default disclosure templates and documentation, but the legal
determination of when AI must be disclosed in your jurisdiction is yours.
```

### 3.4 No outcome guarantee

```
3.4 NO REVENUE, LEAD, OR OUTCOME GUARANTEE

Xpert makes no representation, warranty, or guarantee of:
(a) revenue generated through the Platform;
(b) the volume, quality, or close rate of leads generated;
(c) the number of appointments booked or service outcomes achieved;
(d) any specific business result or return on investment.

Any figures, case studies, or prior-client outcomes shared with you describe past
results under specific circumstances and are not promises of your future performance.
Your results will depend on factors outside Xpert's control — including but not
limited to your offer, your market, your own operational capacity, and your
compliance with applicable law.
```

3.5 LIMITED WARRANTY; DISCLAIMER OF FITNESS FOR PARTICULAR PURPOSE

Except as expressly stated in this Agreement, the Platform is provided "AS IS" and
"AS AVAILABLE". To the maximum extent permitted by applicable law, Xpert disclaims
all other warranties, express or implied, including but not limited to implied

Xpert does not warrant that the Platform will be uninterrupted, error-free, or

### 3.6 Data accuracy

```
3.6 DATA ACCURACY

You are solely responsible for the accuracy, completeness, and currency of all
data you enter or cause to be entered into the Platform, including contact
information, lead sources, consent records, and business details. Xpert is not

### 3.7 Termination for misuse

```
3.7 TERMINATION FOR MISUSE

Xpert may, at its sole discretion and without notice, suspend or terminate your
access to the Platform if Xpert has a reasonable basis to believe you are using
the Platform in connection with:
(a) unsolicited commercial messages sent in violation of CASL, TCPA, or equivalent;
(b) fraud, misrepresentation, or identity theft;
(c) harassment, threats, or intimidation;
(d) illegal activity of any kind;
(e) material breach of this Agreement.

Suspension or termination under this clause does not entitle you to a refund of
fees already paid, and does not relieve you of obligations accrued before

### 3.8 Indemnity

```
3.8 INDEMNIFICATION

You agree to defend, indemnify, and hold harmless Xpert Web Solutions Inc.,
its directors, officers, employees, and agents from and against any claim,
demand, loss, liability, cost, or expense (including reasonable legal fees)
arising out of or related to:

(a) your use of the Platform;
(b) your violation of this Agreement;
(c) your violation of any law applicable to your business or to your end-users;
(d) your failure to obtain or maintain consent from your contacts;
(e) any content, data, or communication you send through the Platform.

This indemnity survives termination of this Agreement.

### 3.9 Limitation of liability

```
3.9 LIMITATION OF LIABILITY

To the maximum extent permitted by applicable law, Xpert's aggregate liability
under this Agreement — in contract, tort, or otherwise — shall not exceed the
total fees you paid to Xpert in the twelve (12) months preceding the event
giving rise to the claim.

Xpert shall not be liable for any indirect, incidental, consequential, special,
punitive, or exemplary damages — including but not limited to lost profits,
lost data, lost business opportunity, or reputational harm — whether or not
Xpert was advised of the possibility of such damages.

Nothing in this clause limits liability for fraud, willful misconduct, or
any liability that cannot be limited by law.
```

### 3.10 Governing law & jurisdiction

```
3.10 GOVERNING LAW; JURISDICTION

This Agreement is governed by and construed under the laws of the Province of
Ontario and the laws of Canada applicable therein, without regard to conflict
of laws principles.

The parties submit to the exclusive jurisdiction of the courts of Ontario,
Canada for any dispute arising under this Agreement, except where a party

jurisdiction of competent authority.

If a client's signed addendum specifies a different governing law, the

subject Xpert to jurisdictions in which Xpert has not expressly agreed.

## 6. AI VOICE / SMS — THE IN-PRODUCT DISCLOSURES

These are what the END-business's AI bots say to the END-user. These are NOT the legal agreement between Xpert and the business — those are separate voice/SMS disclosure templates.

### 6.1 Voice AI opening disclosure (universal default)

```
"Hey, is this {{first_name}}? ... It's {{ai_agent_name}}, the AI assistant working
with {{user_first_name}} at {{company_name}}. Quick heads-up — I'm AI, not a human.
You got a sec?"
```

Voluntary AI disclosure. Builds trust, differentiates from scam calls, and keeps the end-business on the right side of CRTC Telecom Regulatory Policy 2023-150 guidance on AI-generated voice and emerging FTC guidance in the US. Ryan tracks evolving jurisdiction-specific requirements.

### 6.2 SMS opt-in disclosure (before any CEM)

Before any CEM is sent via SMS, the end-user must have an opt-in moment. Default template for a form that generates consent:

```
By entering your phone number, you agree to receive text messages from
{{company_name}} (and its AI assistant) about your inquiry. Message and data
rates may apply. Reply STOP to unsubscribe. Privacy: {{privacy_url}}.
```

This language must be right next to the submit button — not buried in a checkbox that's easy to miss. TCPA enforcement in 2024-2025 emphasized "clear and conspicuous" placement.

### 6.3 Email sender identity footer (CASL mandatory)

Every CEM fires with the following block auto-appended (Eve maintains the template in GHL):

```
{{company_legal_name}}
{{mailing_address}}
{{contact_email}} | {{contact_phone}}

Unsubscribe: {{unsubscribe_link}}
```

Missing any of the four elements = CASL violation = up to $10M per violation.

## 8. NICHE-SPECIFIC RIDERS

Some niches require additional language stacked onto the base agreement.

### 8.1 Mortgage (FSRA)

Mortgage-professional clients sign a rider acknowledging:
- Agent is licensed at level stated (`{{license_number}}`)
- Brokerage of record is named
- No rate quoting, no approval guarantees inside platform-generated communications
- FSRA advertising compliance is agent's responsibility — see `fsra-mortgage-advertising-compliance.md`

### 8.2 Medical / Dental (PHIPA Ontario, HIPAA US)

Medical clients sign:
- Business Associate Agreement (BAA) for US HIPAA-covered entities
- PHIPA information-sharing agreement for Ontario covered entities
- Explicit ban on transmitting Protected Health Information through voice or SMS bots without additional encryption and BAA in place
- Lindsay produces the BAA template

### 8.3 Financial advisory / insurance

- Provincial securities regulation (IIROC, CSA) applies
- Insurance regulation (FSRA insurance side) applies
- Stricter record-keeping obligations
- Lindsay flags every financial-services deal for heightened review before onboarding

### 8.4 Cross-border US clients

- TCPA compliance rider
- State-specific privacy law acknowledgment
- AI voice / auto-dialer restriction acknowledgment (state-variable)

## 10. KNOWN LEGAL RISKS RENÉE SHOULD FLAG TO A LAWYER NOW

These are the items this skill identifies as needing **actual legal review**, not template finalization:

1. **Enforceability of the indemnity (§3.8) against small end-businesses.** Ontario courts have discretion to limit over-broad indemnities. A lawyer must tailor.
2. **Limitation of liability cap (§3.9).** Whether "12 months of fees paid" holds under Ontario law varies by contract type. May need floor exception or different cap basis.
3. **AI-specific liability.** Emerging area. Bill C-27 (Canada AIDA — Artificial Intelligence and Data Act, as of 2024-2025) and EU AI Act both impose new obligations. Lawyer should assess whether Xpert is a "provider" or "deployer" under each, and what obligations follow.
4. **Class-action waiver + arbitration clause.** Not included in this draft because enforceability varies by province. Lawyer should advise.
5. **Auto-renewal + cancellation language.** Ontario Consumer Protection Act restricts auto-renewal terms for certain consumer contracts. B2B likely exempt but confirm.
6. **Cross-border data flow.** If any end-user data leaves Canada (which it likely does — Twilio, OpenAI, Anthropic, etc. have US servers), Quebec Law 25 and federal PIPEDA transfer-impact-assessment requirements kick in. Needs a Data Processing Addendum.
7. **Insurance.** Xpert should carry Errors & Omissions + Cyber Liability insurance. Confirm policy is current and covers AI-specific claims.
8. **White-label liability.** When a partner-agency operates under its own branding atop Easy Deploy, confusion about who is liable to the end-business grows. The partner agreement must spell this out clearly.
9. **Voice cloning / identity representation.** If an end-business's voice AI says "this is {{owner_name}}'s AI assistant" — there's an IP / right-of-publicity exposure if misused. Lawyer should advise on consent + warranty language.
10. **FINTRAC for mortgage business.** If any payment flow through Easy Deploy touches mortgage transactions, FINTRAC reporting obligations may apply. Morgan + Lindsay flag per deal.
