# MORTGAGE SALES WRITING — THE CRAFT

## The principle

**Create desire for the outcome. Stop short of promising it.**

The prospect should feel:
- Something good is happening on their behalf
- They're close to a real outcome
- They'd be smart to keep moving

The regulator / court / underwriter should find:
- Zero promises
- Zero guarantees
- Zero unverifiable claims

**Renée's example that triggered this skill (2026-04-24):**

> "Hi John. I'm just touching base to see where you are with gathering your documents. We have a few lenders in mind but just waiting on you to get the last few things we need. Did you need some help?"

What's working:
- **"We have a few lenders in mind"** — implies progress + value without promising any lender WILL approve
- **"Just waiting on you"** — gentle accountability, positions them as the remaining blocker
- **"Last few things we need"** — suggests we're close, motion is happening
- **"Did you need some help?"** — helper posture, not demand

She dangled the outcome. She didn't commit to it. And she sounded like a human texting a friend, not a corporate compliance robot.

## THE 6 MOVES

### 1. PROGRESS LANGUAGE — create forward motion

✅ YES | ❌ NO
---|---
"We're getting your file in front of a few lenders" | "We've found your lender"
"I've got a couple options in mind for you" | "Your approval is on the way"
"Working on your numbers now" | "You're qualified"
"Pulling together what we need" | "This deal is done"
"Your file is in underwriting with [lender]" | "[Lender] is going to approve you"

### 2. POSSIBILITY LANGUAGE — upside without commitment

✅ YES | ❌ NO
---|---
"There's a real path here" | "You WILL qualify"
"A couple of these could be a fit" | "This lender WILL take you"
"Looking at your scenario, we've got good options worth running" | "Guaranteed approval"
"I think we can land you somewhere between [X]% and [Y]%" | "Your rate is [X]%"
"The numbers look workable" | "You're pre-approved" (unless actually pre-approved in writing)

### 3. SOFT SCARCITY — real urgency, not manufactured

✅ YES | ❌ NO
---|---
"Rates just moved — want to see what's on today?" | "DEAL EXPIRES IN 24 HOURS!!!"
"This lender's program window usually isn't long" | "Only 3 spots left!"
"Things shift fast, let's get you positioned while it's fresh" | "Don't miss out!!!"
"Your current lender's renewal offer probably isn't the best — worth a look" | "You NEED to act NOW"

Soft scarcity is grounded in reality (rates DID move, the window IS usually short). Manufactured urgency is a lie + kills trust.

### 4. HELPER POSTURE — you're on their side

✅ YES | ❌ NO
---|---
"Did you need some help pulling these together?" | "We need these documents by end of day"
"I can walk you through it if any of this is unclear" | "Your application will be canceled without submission"
"Happy to jump on a 10-min call and make it easy" | "Please provide all required documents"
"If anything's confusing, shoot me the question" | "Submit ASAP"

You're a guide with the prospect's hand. Not a gatekeeper.

### 5. SPECIFIC SMALL DETAILS — credibility through knowing the domain

✅ YES | ❌ NO
---|---
"The NOA they ask for is the 2-page one from CRA, not the full return" | "Please provide tax documents"
"For T4s, any of the last 2 years works" | "Submit your financial information"
"Self-employed usually needs 2 years BFS — I'll walk you through what we'll need if that's you" | "Supply proof of income"
"With a 680 credit score + 20% down we've got prime options — with less equity we'd look at alts" | "Your credit impacts your options"

Small details = "this person knows what they're doing." Generic = "this is a call center."

### 5b. EFFORTLESS VISION — let them picture the outcome already in hand

Put the desired outcome in the message as if it's already halfway done — but use SOCIAL PROOF (clients in a similar position), not personal promises. They picture themselves on the other side of the decision through someone else's proven path.

Renée's example (2026-04-24):
> "Hi John, I saw you were interested in refinance mortgage. How soon did you need funding?"

Why it works:
- "How soon did you need funding" skips the "are you going to do this" question entirely
- It assumes the funding is happening — the only question is timing
- The lead mentally answers "well, I'd want it in 30 days" — they just committed in their head
- Zero pressure, zero promise, maximum forward pull

### THE SOCIAL PROOF RULE (Renée 2026-04-24 correction)

**Never say "when YOU get approved"** — that implies a guarantee we can't make. The money isn't ours and the decision isn't ours.

Instead, flip it to social proof: *"we helped clients in a similar position get approved"* + ask a forward-looking question.

YES (social proof + forward pull) | ❌ NO (personal promise)
---|---
"We helped clients in a similar position get approved — they usually put the funds toward [X, Y, Z]. What would be top of mind for you?" | "When you get approved, what are you thinking of using it for?"
"Once clients like you are funded, most close within 30 days — is that timing realistic for you?" | "When we fund your file, you'll close in 30 days"
"For people with your credit + equity profile, we typically see rates in the [X–Y] range — worth running?" | "You'll get a rate around X%"
"Most of our self-employed clients get through with 2 years of BFS — do you have that on hand?" | "You'll qualify with 2 years BFS"

The craft: talk about PAST CLIENTS as the subject, then invite the prospect to compare themselves. They do the picturing without us doing the promising.

### More examples

> "We've helped a lot of refi clients in your spot use the equity for [reno / investment / debt-consolidation]. What's first on your list if the file comes together?"

> "Once clients in your ballpark are funded, they usually spend the first week on [X]. What's your priority when funding lands?"

> "When past clients submit a file like yours, the lender usually comes back in 48–72 hours. I'll update you the second we hear."

**Rule:** in conversations with qualified or near-qualified leads, talk about PAST CLIENTS in "when" — never "when YOU." Let the social proof do the heavy lifting.

This is NOT promising. You're not saying THEY will be approved. You're referencing what happened with similar clients + inviting them to imagine their path forward. If they push back (*"wait, am I even approved?"*) — that's a buying signal + reframe to pre-qualify / pre-approval language.

### 6. TRANSPARENT AVAILABILITY — build trust through presence

✅ YES | ❌ NO
---|---
"I'm finishing up a call with another client, will call you in 10" | "We will respond within 24-48 business hours"
"Tied up on a closing this morning, back to you after 1" | "Our team will reach out"
"Out for lunch — message me anything and I'll tackle it as soon as I'm back" | "An agent will contact you"
"I'm on with my last client — next up is you at 2" | "Please hold for the next available agent"

Real humans tell you where they are. Corporate machines don't.

## MORTGAGE-SPECIFIC COMPLIANCE (FSRA Ontario)

### Must appear on all written marketing (email, web, landing, social, ads)
- Agent name (Renée Ross)
- License level (Mortgage Agent Level 2)
- Brokerage (Ontario Lending Solutions)
- Brokerage license number (13063)

(Pull from `custom_values.user_full_name`, `custom_values.agent_license_level`, `custom_values.brokerage_name`, `custom_values.brokerage_license_number` — never hardcode)

### HARD NO — never use in mortgage copy
- "Best rate in Canada" (unverifiable)
- "Lowest rate" / "cheapest" claims
- Specific rate promises without named lender + written backing
- "Guaranteed approval"
- "100% success rate"
- "Won't affect your credit" (soft pulls vs hard pulls require disclosure)
- "You're pre-approved!" used loosely as a marketing hook when there's no formal lender pre-approval letter backing it up

### IMPORTANT DISTINCTION — pre-qualify vs pre-approval vs approval

All three are real stages in the mortgage process. Use them correctly:

| Term | What it means | When to use it in copy |
|---|---|---|
| **Pre-qualify** | Initial conversational screening — we ask about income, debts, property, credit — no documents pulled, no lender involved yet | "Let me help you get pre-qualified..." / "Fill out this quick pre-qualify form and I'll know in 2 minutes where you stand." |
| **Pre-approval** | Formal letter from a lender based on verified income + credit pull. Valid 90–120 days typically. Tells a buyer how much they can afford so they can shop with confidence. | "Let's get you pre-approved so you know your budget before shopping." / "Your pre-approval letter is ready — I'll email it over." |
| **Approval** | Final commitment from a lender on a specific property once the full file is submitted and underwritten | "Submitting your file to get you approved." / "We're in underwriting with [lender] for final approval." |

**Never conflate these.** A pre-qualify is not a pre-approval. A pre-approval is not a loan approval. When the prospect's in the discovery phase, say pre-qualify. When they're ready to shop, pre-approval. When the deal's live, approval.

### GRAY ZONE — safer not to use
- "Save $X per month" (can be challenged)
- "Approved in 24 hours" (promise-like)
- "You deserve better" (condescending)
- "Act now or miss out" (pressure)

### SAFE TO USE
- "We work with a wide range of lenders"
- "Our job is to find the option that fits your scenario"
- "We've helped clients in situations similar to yours" (if true)
- "Let's see if this makes sense for you"
- "There's a real path here worth exploring"
- "A couple of these could be a fit for you"

## PHRASE BANK — ready-to-use for common mortgage moments

### Inviting a new lead to pre-qualify
> "Hi [first_name], thanks for reaching out. Let me help you get pre-qualified — it's a quick 2-min conversation and I'll tell you exactly where you stand + what kind of options make sense for you. When's a good time to jump on a call?"

### Booking the discovery appointment
> "Hi [first_name], let me book you an appointment with {{custom_values.user_first_name}} to go over your file so we can get your deal submitted. Here's {{custom_values.user_first_name}}'s calendar — grab whatever slot works: {{custom_values.my_calendar}}"

### Moving from pre-qualify to pre-approval
> "Hi [first_name], you look like a solid candidate based on our chat. Next step is getting you a proper pre-approval — that's the letter you'll want in hand before you shop. I'll pull credit + verify income, then we'll have something you can show any realtor. Takes about 24-48 hours once I've got your docs."

### Submitting the file
> "Hi [first_name], got everything I need. Submitting your file to get you approved — I'll message you the second I hear back from the lender."

### Waiting on documents (Renée's original)
> "Hi [first_name], just touching base on getting those last docs together. We've got a couple of lenders in mind but waiting on you for the final pieces. Did you need a hand pulling anything?"

### Application in underwriting
> "Hi [first_name], quick update — your file is in underwriting with [lender]. They usually come back within 48-72 hours. I'll message you the second we hear."

### Missed call / no response after 3 days
> "Hey [first_name], tried reaching you yesterday — no worries if you've been busy. Rates are still in a good spot if you want to keep moving on this. Best number + time for me to try again?"

### After a rate drop
> "Hey [first_name], rates shifted down this morning — want to see what's on today vs where we started?"

### Client is shopping other brokers
> "Totally get wanting to shop it — smart move. Give me one shot to see what we can do with [lender] before you decide. If mine's worse, walk. If it's better, we save you money."

### Partner referral confirmation
> "Got [referred_name]'s info, thanks [partner_name]. I'll call them within the hour and loop you in once the call's done."

### After close (funding complete)
> "Hey [first_name] — we're funded. Keys next [day]. I'll check in around renewal but reach out any time — referrals, future purchases, renewals. Always happy to help."

### Soft decline / not a fit today
> "Hey [first_name], we ran your numbers — things are tight for today's options. A couple months of [specific action: paying down $X, building 3 months of BFS, etc.] and we'd be in a different spot. Want me to check back in [timeframe]?"

### Small loan under $50k (homeowner with equity)
> "Hi [first_name], we got your info. Smaller equity takeouts like yours aren't everyone's wheelhouse but we've got a couple lenders who do this range well. Let me pull together your options and I'll reach out today or tomorrow."

## STYLE CHECKLIST — before sending any mortgage message

Run every draft through this before hitting send:

1. ☐ First name used, no "Dear Customer"
2. ☐ Contractions ("I'm", "we're", "don't") — not "I am" / "we are"
3. ☐ At least one specific, small detail that shows you know the situation
4. ☐ Progress or possibility language present (we're doing X for you OR there's a real path here)
5. ☐ No promised outcome (approval, specific rate, guaranteed close)
6. ☐ No corporate filler ("reach out", "circle back", "kindly", "please be advised", "at your earliest convenience")
7. ☐ Low-pressure close ("talk soon" / "happy to help" / "shoot me a note")
8. ☐ Under 80 words (SMS) / under 180 words (email)
9. ☐ No fake urgency / manufactured scarcity
10. ☐ If marketing (not 1:1): FSRA compliance line present (agent + license + brokerage + brokerage license #)

## WHAT NEVER WORKS — delete on sight

- ALL CAPS (reads as spam)
- Exclamation clusters ("!!!", "!!!!!!")
- Money emoji overload 💰💵💸
- Manufactured urgency without a real reason ("ACT NOW")
- Corporate voice in SMS ("Dear Valued Client")
- Rate promises without named lender + written backing
- "You deserve" (condescending)
- "Fast, easy, guaranteed" (triple promise = triple risk)

## AUDIENCE ADJUSTMENTS

### Direct consumer (the borrower)
Warm, personal, contraction-heavy, helper posture. Renée's own voice.

### Partner / realtor / referral source
Professional but still human. They're your peer, not your prospect. Emphasize: we handle the work, you stay informed, you earn on the close.

### Voice AI greeting / prompt
Even shorter. Under 25 words on the open. No corporate script language. Always include CRTC disclosure ("This call may be recorded and handled by an AI assistant") as the non-negotiable first line.
