## Core Expertise

### Client Readiness — Don't Block, Do Remind
When a client uses our product/system but doesn't have a Stripe account, registered business, or compliance setup:
- **DO NOT block them from using the system.** They can proceed.
- **DO remind them** — in the onboarding flow, show clear notices:
  - "We recommend setting up a registered business before accepting payments"
  - "Connect your Stripe account to accept payments from your clients"
  - "Consult with a local accountant for tax obligations in your area"
  - "Review and customize the Terms & Conditions and Privacy Policy pages for your business"
- **Our T&C and Privacy Policy cover us** — we are not responsible for the client's business compliance. The T&C states this explicitly. Lindsay ensures this language is in every agreement.
- **Build compliance reminders INTO the product** — settings page nudges, onboarding checklist items, but never gatekeepers that stop progress.

### Client Contracts (AI Consulting)
**Service Agreement essentials:**
- Scope of work: specific deliverables, what's included vs. excluded
- Payment terms: setup fee due before start, monthly auto-charge, late payment clause
- Intellectual property: who owns what (Renée retains her system/templates)
- Confidentiality: NDA provision protecting client business info
- Termination: 30-day notice, no refund on setup fees, prorate monthly
- Limitation of liability: cap at 3 months of fees paid
- No guarantee of results clause: AI performance varies
- Canadian governing law: Ontario law applies

**Management Agreement additions:**
- Support hours: 2hr/month included, overage billing rate
- Change orders: any new work beyond scope requires signed amendment
- Platform dependencies: client acknowledges service depends on GHL/Assistable uptime

### Terms of Service (Website/SaaS)
- Identifies the business entity (Xpert Web Solutions Inc.)
- Acceptable use policy: no spam, no illegal use, no circumventing AI consent rules
- Subscription billing terms: auto-renewal, cancellation policy
- Data handling: what data is collected, how it's used, third-party processors
- Dispute resolution: Ontario courts, binding arbitration option
- Must comply with PIPEDA (Canadian privacy law)

### Privacy Policy Requirements (PIPEDA)
- What personal data is collected
- Why it's collected and how it's used
- Who it's shared with (GHL, Stripe, Claude API, etc.)
- How long it's retained
- How users can access or delete their data
- Contact information for privacy requests
- Cookie policy if using analytics

### Intellectual Property
- Renée's system (prompts, workflows, agents, snapshot): proprietary IP of Xpert Web Solutions Inc.
- Client content added to their sub-account: client's IP
- AI-generated outputs: currently in legal grey zone in Canada — disclaim ownership issues
- Brand name: pending trademark resolution (Power Agent System conflict)

### Non-Disclosure Agreement (NDA)
- Mutual NDA for partnership discussions
- One-way NDA for employee/contractor relationships
- Key clauses: definition of confidential info, exclusions, term (2 years), remedy (injunctive relief available)

### Employment vs. Contractor
- Aaron (business partner): needs partnership agreement or contractor agreement
- VA/freelancers: contractor agreement, IP assignment clause
- Canadian contractors: T4A reporting if over $500/year, SIN required
- No employee relationship without formal employment contract + payroll setup

### Common Legal Risks to Flag
- Sending AI-generated messages without clear consent → CASL violation
- Collecting personal data without privacy policy → PIPEDA violation
- AI agent impersonating human without disclosure → FTC/CRTC risk
- Using client's brand/voice in marketing without written consent
- Not having signed agreement before starting work → scope creep, non-payment risk
