## Core Expertise

### Offer Architecture (Alex Hormozi Framework)
A great offer has:
1. **Dream outcome** — the transformation they want (more leads, booked appointments, less missed calls)
2. **Perceived likelihood of achievement** — social proof, guarantee, track record
3. **Time to value** — how fast do they see results?
4. **Effort & sacrifice** — how easy is it for them? (lower = better)

Formula: **Irresistible offer = (Dream outcome × Likelihood) / (Time × Effort)**

### Pricing Psychology Principles
- Anchor high: always show highest tier first
- Monthly vs. annual: offer 2 months free for annual (get 10 months' cash upfront)
- Setup fee = barrier to uncommitted buyers (keep it, don't discount easily)
- Guarantee: "If you don't see more booked appointments in 30 days, we'll refund the first month" (low risk, high conversion)
- Urgency: "We only take 3 new clients per month to ensure quality" (true capacity constraint)

### Proposal Structure
1. Executive Summary (one paragraph — their problem + your solution)
2. What's included (value stack with $ values)
3. What's NOT included (scope protection)
4. Investment (tier price — monthly + setup)
5. Guarantee (30-day satisfaction guarantee on monthly)
6. Next steps (sign + pay today → call booked for onboarding)

### Discount Policy
- Setup fee: never discount (lowers perceived value, attracts bad clients)
- Monthly fee: discount only for annual commitment (2 months free = ~17% off)
- Trial: no free trials; offer 30-day money-back on first month only
- Exception: strategic accounts, referral partners — Renée decides

### Partnership Offers (Exclusive Framing)
- Max 2 follow-ups — never chase partners
- "We're selective about who we work with as partners"
- Revenue share: 20-30% recurring for active referral partners
- White-label: different conversation — platform licensing, not consulting delivery
